1. RESEARCH QUESTION & METHOD
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Core Question:
──────────────
How does time pressure affect decision-making quality in e-commerce contexts?

Human Factors Perspective:
──────────────────────────
• Time constraints force cognitive trade-offs (System 1 vs System 2 thinking)
• Information processing under pressure = heuristics + selective attention
• Bounded rationality in digital interfaces

Experimental Design:
────────────────────
• Within-subject design: 3 time conditions (6s, 15s, 30s)
• 10 Le Bon Coin smartphone listings (€299-€1099)
• Task: Select the most interesting product after each exposure
• N = 33 participants, 80 valid trials

Measured Variables:
───────────────────
• Product selected (price, brand, condition)
• Declared motivations (7 factors: price, brand, condition, seller, location, 
  newness, high-price signal)
• Multiple selections allowed to capture decision complexity

2. KEY FINDINGS - 5 VISUALIZATIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Graph 1: Motivation Evolution by Duration
──────────────────────────────────────────
What it shows: Frequency of each motivation factor across 6s/15s/30s conditions

Human Factors Insight:
• Tests whether time enables deeper information processing
• Expected: Shift from heuristic cues (price, brand) to analytical factors 
  (seller reputation, condition details)
• Reveals cognitive load management strategies under time pressure

Graph 2: Price Distribution Analysis
─────────────────────────────────────
What it shows: Mean price + variance across time conditions

Human Factors Insight:
• Does time improve value assessment or create over-analysis?
• Two scenarios:
  - Ascending price = Users appreciate quality with more time
  - Descending price = Users optimize for deals with more time
• Variance shows decision confidence (low variance = consensus)

Graph 3: Condition Preference (State Quality)
──────────────────────────────────────────────
What it shows: Distribution of "Good / Very Good / Excellent" selections

Human Factors Insight:
• Quality-price trade-off tolerance
• Is "excellent condition" a prerequisite or negotiable factor?
• Informs information hierarchy in product cards

Graph 4: Overall Motivation Ranking
────────────────────────────────────
What it shows: Global frequency of all 7 motivation factors

Human Factors Insight:
• Reveals natural decision criteria hierarchy
• Price dominance would suggest high price sensitivity
• Guides information architecture prioritization

Graph 5: Time × Average Price Correlation
──────────────────────────────────────────
What it shows: Linear relationship between exposure time and mean selected price

Human Factors Insight:
• Direct test of time pressure effect on economic choices
• Flat curve = Time doesn't affect value perception (other factors dominate)
• Curved = Time enables better quality assessment or bargain hunting


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
3. EXPECTED RESULTS & UX IMPLICATIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Hypothesis 1: Cognitive Complexity Increase
────────────────────────────────────────────
6s → Simple heuristics (price, brand recognition)
15s → Multi-criteria comparison emerges  
30s → Holistic evaluation with 3-4 factors

→ UX Implication: Design for "fast mode" (price + brand prominent) vs 
  "comparison mode" (detailed comparison tools)

Hypothesis 2: Diminishing Returns of Time
──────────────────────────────────────────
Optimal decision time ≈ 15 seconds
Beyond 15s: marginal improvement or over-analysis paralysis

→ UX Implication: Encourage 10-15s engagement, not more (progressive disclosure)

Hypothesis 3: Price Sensitivity Dominates
──────────────────────────────────────────
"Price low" selected in 60%+ of trials regardless of time

→ UX Implication: Price is THE primary information - maximize its visibility
  and clarity across all interfaces

Human Factors Framework:
────────────────────────
This study bridges:
• Attention & perception (what users notice under time pressure)
• Decision-making (how choices change with cognitive load)
• Applied ergonomics (optimizing digital interfaces for real-world constraints)

Quantitative UX Contribution:
─────────────────────────────
• Moves beyond qualitative "users prefer X" to "time pressure changes decision 
  criteria by Y%"
• Data-driven design recommendations (not opinions)
• Reproducible methodology for A/B testing validation

CONCLUSION (30 seconds)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

This Phase 3 study quantifies the trade-off between speed and decision quality
in e-commerce, using a controlled time manipulation paradigm.

By measuring both behavioral outcomes (product selected) and cognitive processes
(declared motivations), we can design interfaces that:
1. Optimize information hierarchy for fast decisions
2. Support deeper engagement when users have time
3. Adapt to user behavior patterns (time on page)

The human factors approach ensures our UX recommendations are grounded in 
cognitive psychology, not just aesthetic preferences.
